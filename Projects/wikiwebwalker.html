wikiwebwalker

As my time at UMass Amherst was coming to a close, I decided to take a course on Natural Language Processing. I had been interested in the topic for a while, but I had no experience with it. I was excited to learn more about how computers can understand and generate human language. Luckily, I found myself in the same class as some friends I made back during my freshman year, Jasmine Cai and Jacob Grassi. We decided to team up for the final project, and we came up with the idea of creating a tool that could solve The Wiki Game. Jasmine wrote a wonderful article on her blog about the project, which includes an easy-to-red step-by-step process of our development and the technical details of our solution. In addition, this formal paper was submitted to our professor with our final results and conclusions.
